## SECTION 3: ADJACENCY MATRIX & SPATIAL RELATIONSHIPS

### 3.1 Adjacency Tier System

TIER 1: IMMEDIATE ADJACENCY (Direct Connection Required)

| Room Pair | Why | Priority |
|-----------|-----|----------|
| Kitchen ↔ Dining | Food service workflow | CRITICAL |
| Dining ↔ Living | Social flow, entertaining | HIGH |
| Master Bedroom ↔ Ensuite | Convenience, privacy | CRITICAL |
| Entry ↔ Kitchen | Grocery flow, daily routine | HIGH |

TIER 2: IMPORTANT ADJACENCY (Same Zone)

| Room Pair | Why | Priority |
|-----------|-----|----------|
| Bedrooms ↔ Bathrooms | User convenience | HIGH |
| Living ↔ Entry | Natural arrival sequence | MEDIUM |
| Kitchen ↔ Pantry | User convinience | HIGH |
| Bathroom ↔ Utility | User convinience | MEDIUM |
| Living ↔ Parking | Natural arrival sequence | HIGH |

TIER 4: MUST BE SEPARATED

| Room Pair | Why | Priority |
|-----------|-----|----------|
| Bedrooms ↔ Living/Entertainment | Sleep protection from noise | CRITICAL |
| Bedrooms ↔ Kitchen | Odor/noise management | HIGH |
| Bathrooms ↔ Living Rooms | Privacy, odor management | HIGH |

### 3.2 Negative Adjacencies (Must AVOID)

| Room Pair | Problem | Solution |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Kitchen ↔ Bedrooms | Odor, noise, heat | Separate by 5m+ |
| Bathrooms/Utility ↔ Eating Areas | Odor, psychological discomfort | Separate by 5m+ |

### 3.3 Mandatory Adjacency Rules

- Each bathroom MUST be adjacent to at least one bedroom in the adjacencies list
- Living room MUST be adjacent to kitchen
- Living room MUST be adjacent to at least 2 bedrooms (it is the circulation hub — no corridor needed)
- Utility must be adjacent to bathroom

## SECTION 5: WINDOW REQUIREMENTS

### 5.1 Window-to-Floor Area Ratios

| Light Level | Window Ratio | Best For |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| Comfortable | 10-15% | Most residential rooms |
| Optimal | 15-20% | Living, dining, offices |

Room Examples:
- 10m² room: 1-2m² windows
- 20m² room: 2-4m² windows
- 30m² room: 3-6m² windows

### 5.2 Window Orientation Strategy (Northern Hemisphere)

| Direction | Best For |
|-----------|----------|
| South-facing | Living, dining, family areas |
| East-facing | Bedrooms, offices |
| North-facing | Studies, art rooms |

### 5.3 Ventilation Requirements

- Operable windows/doors: At least 4% of floor area per room
- Minimum 1 opening in each habitable room

## SECTION 10: SPECIFICATION AGENT WORKFLOW

### 10.1 Input Processing Steps

1. **Identify rooms** from user request — count bedrooms, note any special rooms
2. **Assign target areas** using Section 2.1 standards scaled to total home size
3. **Set adjacencies** using Section 3 tier system — always list bedroom↔bathroom and kitchen↔living pairs
4. **Add doors** — every room needs at least one door; main entrance faces street/exterior; parking has an exterior door and an interior door
5. **Add windows** — use 10-20% of floor area per room; orient per Section 5.2 if direction known
6. **Verify total area** — sum of all room areas must equal stated total_area (±5%)
7. **Living room area check** — living room must be ≥ 18% of total area

### 10.2 Critical Constraints

- **NO corridor** unless user explicitly asked for one
- **Living room, bathroom, kitchen mandatory** — always include them
- **Each bathroom adjacent to a bedroom** — enforce in adjacencies list
- **Total area must be consistent** — sum of room target_area values must match total_area
